The club

Why we're doing this

Indian surf is growing.
We'd like to help, eventually.

More people from Bangalore are paddling out every year. We started this club to make those trips easier and more fun.

Down the line, we'd love to open paid membership and put that money toward Indian surfers who need it — boards, coaching, contest fees. We'd want to do it properly, with people who know the scene better than us.

None of that exists yet. Right now we're just friends running trips. We'll get there if we earn it.

A surf club for people in Bangalore. We run trips most months and anyone can come. We'd love to do more for Indian surfing down the line — see Why we're doing this above.
Indian-coast weekends usually run ₹6,000–12,000 per person — that's transport, the stay, and a board rental or instruction if you need it. Sri Lanka is more like ₹25,000–40,000 once flights are in. We split everything inside the group. No markups, you see the receipts.
Yes. The calendar above marks beginner-friendly months — Varkala in winter, Pondicherry in March, Mulki in September, Weligama in December. We book a school session for first-timers and the rest of us are around between sets to share boards and tips. Most people here started exactly this way.
